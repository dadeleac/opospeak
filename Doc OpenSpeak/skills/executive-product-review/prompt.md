# Executive Product Review Skill

## Purpose

Provide a high-level executive assessment of the overall product quality, strategic coherence, long-term sustainability and release maturity of the application.

This review is not a replacement for specialized audits.

Instead, it synthesizes:
- UX findings;
- architecture findings;
- accessibility findings;
- monetization findings;
- retention findings;
- performance findings;
- operational findings;

into a unified product evaluation.

The objective is to determine:

- whether the product is strategically coherent;
- whether the product identity remains intact;
- whether the current trajectory is healthy;
- whether the product is evolving sustainably;
- what the highest-leverage priorities are;
- what risks threaten long-term success.

This review should aggressively identify:
- strategic incoherence;
- product drift;
- emotional fragmentation;
- complexity accumulation;
- unsustainable growth patterns;
- quality dilution.

The review must think like:
- a product owner;
- a design director;
- a systems architect;
- a long-term product steward.

Not like:
- a feature reviewer.

# Core Review Philosophy

A strong product is not:
- a collection of features;
- a technically impressive system;
- a trend-driven interface;
- an engagement machine.

A strong product is:
- coherent;
- intentional;
- emotionally stable;
- strategically focused;
- sustainable;
- trustworthy.

The review should optimize for:
- long-term product integrity;
not short-term excitement.

The product should feel:
- crafted around a clear philosophy.

# Core Evaluation Areas

## 1. Product Identity Coherence

Evaluate whether:
- the product has a clear identity;
- the experience feels emotionally unified;
- the design, UX and monetization reinforce the same philosophy.

Detect:
- fragmented identity;
- feature-driven direction;
- inconsistent emotional tone;
- trend-chasing;
- conflicting priorities.

The product should feel:
- recognizable and intentional.

---

## 2. Strategic Product Focus

Evaluate whether the roadmap and current implementation remain focused on:
- core value;
- differentiation;
- emotional relevance;
- sustainable simplicity.

Detect:
- feature creep;
- roadmap dilution;
- weak differentiation;
- reactive development;
- complexity accumulation.

Strong products say:
- “no”
frequently.

---

## 3. Premium Product Quality

Evaluate whether the application feels:
- premium;
- refined;
- mature;
- emotionally calm;
- detail-oriented.

Review:
- UX consistency;
- polish level;
- onboarding quality;
- visual maturity;
- interaction quality;
- emotional sophistication.

Detect:
- rough edges;
- inconsistent polish;
- emotionally cheap patterns;
- dashboard syndrome;
- generic UX.

Premium quality emerges from restraint and consistency.

---

## 4. Emotional & Psychological Integrity

Evaluate whether the product:
- respects the user;
- avoids manipulation;
- preserves calmness;
- supports emotional trust.

Detect:
- pressure mechanics;
- addictive patterns;
- emotionally noisy UX;
- guilt-based engagement;
- manipulative monetization.

A wellbeing-oriented product must remain emotionally safe.

---

## 5. Long-Term Sustainability

Evaluate whether the product can realistically sustain:
- years of iteration;
- healthy retention;
- maintainable architecture;
- product coherence;
- operational stability.

Detect:
- unsustainable complexity;
- fragile architecture;
- roadmap explosion;
- emotional dilution;
- operational overload.

Sustainability matters more than launch hype.

---

## 6. Retention & Attachment Potential

Evaluate whether the product can realistically become:
- a trusted ritual;
- emotionally meaningful;
- part of users' routines.

Review:
- onboarding quality;
- emotional resonance;
- repeatability;
- calmness;
- long-term value.

Detect:
- novelty-only engagement;
- emotionally shallow interactions;
- fatigue risk;
- weak habit integration.

The strongest products become:
- emotionally relevant.

---

## 7. Monetization & Trust Alignment

Evaluate whether monetization:
- supports premium positioning;
- preserves trust;
- aligns emotionally with the product.

Detect:
- monetization harming calmness;
- aggressive upsells;
- pricing incoherence;
- trust erosion risks.

Monetization should feel:
- supportive of the product;
not imposed onto it.

---

## 8. Product Maturity & Release Readiness

Evaluate whether the product feels:
- production-ready;
- stable;
- intentional;
- publicly trustworthy.

Review:
- UX maturity;
- operational maturity;
- polish consistency;
- accessibility maturity;
- performance stability.

Detect:
- beta-quality experiences;
- unfinished areas;
- operational fragility;
- inconsistent quality levels.

The product should feel:
- dependable.

---

## 9. Product Risk Assessment

Identify:
- strategic risks;
- UX risks;
- emotional risks;
- scalability risks;
- retention risks;
- monetization risks;
- operational risks.

Prioritize:
- risks with highest long-term impact.

Not all risks matter equally.

---

## 10. Founder/Product Stewardship Quality

Evaluate whether product decisions reflect:
- intentional stewardship;
- product discipline;
- emotional clarity;
- quality prioritization.

Detect:
- reactive iteration;
- overbuilding;
- trend-driven choices;
- fear-driven feature accumulation.

Great products are curated carefully.

# Severity Levels

## Critical
Strongly threatens long-term product quality, trust or sustainability.

## High
Noticeably harms product coherence or strategic direction.

## Medium
Moderate strategic or experiential weakness.

## Low
Minor product refinement opportunity.

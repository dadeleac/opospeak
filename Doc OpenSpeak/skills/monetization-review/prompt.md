# Monetization Review Skill

## Purpose

Evaluate whether the application's monetization strategy:
- aligns with the product identity;
- preserves trust;
- supports long-term sustainability;
- feels emotionally coherent;
- reinforces premium positioning.

This review must not focus only on conversion optimization.

The objective is to determine whether monetization:
- feels fair;
- respects the user;
- supports retention;
- preserves emotional calmness;
- strengthens perceived value;
- avoids damaging the product experience.

The review should aggressively identify:
- manipulative monetization;
- premature paywalls;
- trust erosion;
- emotional pressure;
- feature gating mistakes;
- pricing incoherence;
- retention-destructive monetization patterns.

The monetization system should feel:
- aligned with the product;
not extracted from it.

# Core Review Philosophy

Monetization should:
- reinforce trust;
- reinforce quality perception;
- reinforce emotional attachment.

Monetization should not:
- create pressure;
- create anxiety;
- exploit emotional vulnerability;
- interrupt calmness;
- damage long-term retention.

Premium monetization feels:
- fair;
- understandable;
- emotionally coherent;
- respectful.

The user should feel:
- invited;
not cornered.

# Core Evaluation Areas

## 1. Product-Monetization Alignment

Evaluate whether monetization feels naturally connected to:
- the product philosophy;
- emotional positioning;
- user expectations;
- premium identity.

Detect:
- monetization copied from generic SaaS apps;
- emotionally incoherent paywalls;
- aggressive conversion-first flows;
- “growth hack” feeling;
- monetization that breaks calmness.

The monetization model should feel native to the product.

---

## 2. Perceived Fairness

Evaluate:
- pricing fairness;
- value clarity;
- feature distribution;
- trial honesty;
- free-tier usefulness.

Detect:
- deceptive pricing;
- artificial limitations;
- exploitative gating;
- confusing plans;
- hidden restrictions;
- “bait and trap” dynamics.

Users should feel:
- respected;
not manipulated.

---

## 3. Paywall Timing & Placement

Evaluate:
- when paywalls appear;
- emotional context;
- interruption level;
- trust impact;
- onboarding impact.

Detect:
- paywalls before value delivery;
- onboarding monetization pressure;
- excessive upgrade prompts;
- emotionally awkward interruptions;
- monetization before attachment.

The user should understand the value before being asked to pay.

---

## 4. Premium Positioning

Evaluate whether premium feels:
- desirable;
- coherent;
- emotionally aspirational;
- quality-driven.

Review:
- visual presentation;
- premium messaging;
- feature framing;
- perceived craftsmanship.

Detect:
- generic premium messaging;
- weak differentiation;
- feature quantity positioning;
- “unlock everything” framing;
- discount-driven perception.

Premium should feel:
- meaningful;
not transactional.

---

## 5. Emotional Safety & Trust

Evaluate whether monetization:
- preserves calmness;
- avoids pressure;
- avoids guilt;
- avoids anxiety;
- respects user autonomy.

Detect:
- countdown timers;
- fake urgency;
- manipulative scarcity;
- emotional guilt mechanics;
- retention pressure;
- dark patterns.

A wellbeing product must never monetize emotional vulnerability.

---

## 6. Subscription Strategy Quality

Evaluate:
- pricing clarity;
- annual/monthly balance;
- trial structure;
- cancellation expectations;
- perceived commitment level.

Detect:
- confusing pricing structures;
- psychologically aggressive annual pushes;
- hidden subscription complexity;
- unclear renewal expectations.

Subscriptions should feel:
- transparent;
- low-anxiety;
- trustworthy.

---

## 7. Long-Term Sustainability

Evaluate whether the monetization strategy can realistically support:
- sustainable revenue;
- sustainable product evolution;
- long-term retention;
- premium positioning.

Detect:
- monetization dependent on manipulation;
- unsustainable free tiers;
- pricing too weak for sustainability;
- weak premium incentives.

The strategy should support:
- healthy business;
- healthy users.

---

## 8. Free Experience Quality

Evaluate whether free users still receive:
- meaningful value;
- emotional respect;
- coherent experience;
- calm interaction.

Detect:
- intentionally degraded free UX;
- frustration-based conversion;
- constant upsell pressure;
- “demo app” feeling.

Free users should still trust the product.

---

## 9. Conversion Psychology

Evaluate:
- emotional readiness for upgrade;
- perceived value progression;
- attachment before monetization;
- upgrade motivation.

Detect:
- conversion attempts before trust;
- weak emotional payoff;
- feature-first selling;
- lack of perceived transformation.

The strongest conversions come from:
- trust;
- attachment;
- perceived value.

---

## 10. Brand & App Store Perception

Evaluate whether monetization:
- strengthens premium perception;
- supports App Store reputation;
- aligns with premium indie positioning.

Detect:
- cheap-feeling monetization;
- excessive upgrade prompts;
- visual inconsistency;
- monetization harming reviews.

Monetization affects:
- perceived product maturity;
- trustworthiness;
- emotional reputation.

The review should optimize for:

- emotional coherence;
- healthy conversion.

# Monetization Philosophy

The goal is not:
- maximum short-term conversion;
- aggressive upselling;
- compulsive subscriptions.

The goal is:
- sustainable trust;
- premium positioning;
- healthy retention;
- emotionally coherent revenue.

Users should feel:
- happy to support the product;
not pressured into paying.
